TIGERS

They're Amazing!

Tigers are the largest cat species in the world and third largest land carnivore, with Siberian Tigers, pictured above, weighing up to 660 pounds! They typically live to be about 10-15 years, but a few have been known to live up to 26 years.


Tigers' stripes are like human fingerprints - every tiger's are unique! They're also the only cat to be completely striped, meaning they have striped skin.


You might be surprised to find out that tigers aren't great hunters. The estimate is that a tiger is successful only 1 in 10 or 20 attempts. They'll hunt any time of day but are best at night because of their strong night vision. While not great at hunting, they're fantastic swimmers. They'll swim pretty far to hunt or cross rivers, and the cubs play in water too. They mostly eat deer, boar, buffalo, and antelope. They aren't picky though!


Female tigers are also awesome mamas! They usually give birth to two or three blind cubs and raise and protect them for two years. After that, tigers are solitary for the most part. Check out the video below!
